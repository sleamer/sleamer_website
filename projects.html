Projects and Solutions

I often find myself presented with a projects that require a little unconventional thinking and hard work. The key is that it doesn't matter if it is technology or art, I love navigating unknown waters.

Below are examples of problems and how I arrived at solutions.







Creative Case 1: Retail Train

Trader Joe's needed to showcase holiday products while making a visual impact to draw in children and their parents. It also needed to be inexpensive.

I gathered most supplies from the store: a cast off wine barrel, old riveteering (shelves), scraps of cardboard, and a few extra store bells to create the bulk of the train. The extra "bells & whistles" are ceiling medallions (wheels) led lights (it lit up!) and a smoke machine.

Creative Case 2: Yelm WA

From a collective goal of turning Yelm, WA into a tourist town, business owners sought out an artist who could address their business goals while creating a cohesive look and feel.


Creative Case 3: Store Standards and Art Fixes

There was a need to standardize a theme and art quick fixes for Trader Joes such that all art team members could reference them. This also helped management visualize the store's art marketing goals.

Creative Case 4: Business Website

This project's process has been rather straightforward, but has required mobile first developement and coordination with multiple remote stakeholders via basecamp.

Creative Case 5: Artist Website

This artist need a very minimal site that was easy for her to update. I investigated and used a flatfile cms (StaceyApp) to build the project. While not complicated, sometimes simple solutions are the best.
